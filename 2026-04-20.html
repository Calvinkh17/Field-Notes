TEC — Sunday, April 19, 2026

Serving Saints

The Adulting Church, Part 2
Preacher: Sean / Text: Ephesians 4:12 / Series: The Adulting Church
The Text
"to equip the saints for the work of ministry, for building up the body of Christ" (Ephesians 4:12, ESV)

Paul's vision for the church is breathtaking in its clarity and scope. The gifted leaders—apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors and teachers—aren't called to do all the ministry themselves. Rather, they exist to equip the saints, the ordinary believers, for the work of ministry. This isn't about creating a professional clergy class while everyone else spectates. This is about unleashing the entire body of Christ for gospel work.

The goal is corporate: building up the body of Christ. Each saint, equipped and engaged, contributes to the maturation and strengthening of the whole. When we serve one another in love, we're participating in Christ's own building project. He's the one constructing His church, but He uses us—equipped saints—as His instruments in this sacred work.

Notice the beautiful progression: gifted leaders equip saints, equipped saints do ministry, ministry builds up the body. This isn't just individual spiritual growth, though that matters. This is about the church becoming what Christ intends: a mature, unified, love-driven community that reflects His own character to the world.

Devotional Angle — For You, Right Now

Calvin, you're living this text in real time. As you lead Choice Men, you're not just facilitating discussions or managing logistics—you're equipping saints for ministry. Those men in your group aren't just recipients of your leadership; they're being prepared by God through you to serve others, to build up the body. Your faithfulness in this role matters more than you know.

As you prepare for eldership, this passage should recalibrate your understanding of leadership entirely. Pastoral leadership isn't about being the super-Christian who does all the spiritual heavy lifting. It's about creating other ministers, multiplying servants, unleashing the gifts that already exist in the body. The elders at TEC aren't trying to do all the ministry—they're trying to equip you and others to do it.

Your approaching fatherhood adds another layer here. You'll soon be equipping your own child for a lifetime of ministry and service. The same principle applies: you're not called to be your child's only spiritual influence, but to prepare them to serve Christ and His people throughout their life. Start thinking now about how you'll shape a heart for service, not just personal holiness.

And consider your marriage: how are you and your wife equipping each other for ministry? How is your household becoming a launching pad for service rather than an insular retreat? The saints being equipped in Ephesians 4:12 include both of you. Your family life should contribute to the building up of Christ's body, not detract from it.

Questions to Sit With
1.
How are you currently being equipped for ministry, and how are you actively equipping others?
2.
What would change about your leadership of Choice Men if you truly saw your role as equipping these men for their own ministries?
3.
How will your approaching eldership and fatherhood require you to shift from doing ministry to multiplying ministers?
4.
What specific ways could your household contribute to building up the body of Christ rather than just maintaining itself?

A Prayer to Close

Father, thank You for equipping me through Your Word and Your people for the work of ministry. Help me see my roles—as husband, future father, group leader, and aspiring elder—as opportunities to equip others rather than platforms for my own significance. Give me wisdom to multiply ministers rather than just manage tasks. May my life contribute to the building up of Your body, and may I never lose sight of the corporate vision You have for Your church. Use me to prepare others for a lifetime of faithful service to You.

Today's Action: During lunch and the afternoon singing, intentionally encourage one person in their gifts and discuss how they might use those gifts to serve the body.
