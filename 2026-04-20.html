Serving Saints

The Adulting Church, Part 2
Preacher: Sean / Text: Ephesians 4:12 / Series: The Adulting Church
The Text
"to equip the saints for the work of ministry, for building up the body of Christ" (Ephesians 4:12)

Paul's vision of the church is breathtakingly practical. Christ has given gifts to His people—apostles, prophets, evangelists, shepherds and teachers—not to do all the ministry themselves, but to equip the saints for the work of ministry. The Greek word for "equip" (katartismos) carries the idea of mending nets, setting bones, or preparing soldiers for battle. It's thorough, intentional preparation for real work.

This isn't about creating spiritual consumers who sit back and watch the professionals. Every saint has work to do in building up the body of Christ. The pastor-teachers equip, but the saints do the ministry. The goal is corporate: building up the body of Christ until we all reach maturity, until we're no longer tossed about by every wind of doctrine.

Ministry isn't the exclusive domain of the ordained. It's the calling of every believer who has been equipped and sent to serve. The church grows and matures when every member is actively building up the body in love.

Devotional Angle — For You, Right Now

Calvin, this text hits directly at the heart of your calling as a leader in Choice Men and as an aspiring elder. You're not just consuming ministry; you're in the business of equipping others for ministry. Every time you lead that discipleship group, you're living out Ephesians 4:12. You're taking what God has given you and using it to prepare other men for the work of building up the body.

But here's the challenge: are you equipping the Choice Men to do ministry, or are you doing all the ministry for them? There's a difference between being the guy who has all the answers and being the guy who helps others discover how to find and apply God's truth themselves. Your role isn't to be the permanent spiritual father to these men—it's to help them become spiritual fathers to others.

As you prepare for eldership, this principle becomes even more crucial. Elders don't just shepherd; they equip others to shepherd. They don't just teach; they raise up teachers. With a baby coming and increased responsibilities on the horizon, you'll need to multiply yourself through others rather than trying to be indispensable.

Consider your marriage too. How are you equipping Sarah for ministry? How are you preparing to equip your child for a life of service to Christ? The same principle applies in your home—you're not just the spiritual leader who does all the spiritual work, but the one who equips others in your household for their own calling to build up the body of Christ.

Questions to Sit With
1.
In what ways might I be doing ministry for people instead of equipping them to do ministry themselves?
2.
How can I better prepare the Choice Men to lead others rather than just follow me?
3.
What specific ministry gifts do I see in the men I'm discipling, and how can I help develop those gifts?
4.
How will becoming a father change my approach to equipping others for ministry?

A Prayer to Close
Lord Jesus, thank You for giving gifts to Your church for the purpose of equipping the saints. Help me to be faithful in preparing others for ministry rather than trying to do it all myself. Give me wisdom to see the gifts You've placed in the men I lead and the courage to release them into their own calling. As I prepare for eldership and fatherhood, teach me to multiply disciples who will multiply disciples. May Your body be built up in love through the work You've called each of us to do.
Today: Identify one specific way you can equip rather than do ministry for someone in your sphere of influence this week.
