THE TEXT
Ephesians 4:12
"to equip the saints for the work of ministry, for building up the body of Christ"

Paul's vision for the church is breathtaking in its simplicity and revolutionary in its implications. The apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors and teachers mentioned in verse 11 are not called to do all the ministry themselves. Rather, they exist to equip the saints—every believer—for the work of ministry. This is the pathway to maturity that Christ has ordained for His church.

The word "equip" carries the idea of preparing, mending, or making complete. It's the same word used for mending fishing nets—taking something that's torn or incomplete and restoring it to full functionality. Every saint needs this equipping, and every equipped saint is called to the work of ministry. The goal is clear: building up the body of Christ until we all reach unity in the faith and mature manhood, measured by nothing less than the fullness of Christ Himself.

This isn't about professional ministers doing ministry while others watch. This is about the whole body functioning as God designed it, with each part contributing to the health and growth of the whole. When saints are equipped and engaged in ministry, the church builds itself up in love—exactly what Paul describes in verse 16.

DEVOTIONAL ANGLE — FOR YOU, RIGHT NOW

Calvin, this text hits right at the center of your calling as a leader in Christ's church. You're leading the Choice Men group not as an end in itself, but as part of Christ's grand strategy to equip saints for ministry. Every conversation you have with those men, every truth you help them see, every way you model faithfulness—this is all part of the equipping work that builds up the body.

As you step toward eldership, you're not stepping toward a position where you do all the ministry while others remain passive. You're moving toward a role where you help unleash the ministry potential of every believer in your care. The goal isn't to create dependence on your wisdom or leadership, but to equip others to do the work of ministry themselves. This changes everything about how you lead—both now and in whatever future roles the Lord has for you.

Your growing family is also a ministry context. As a husband and soon-to-be father, you're called to equip your wife and children for their own works of ministry. Your home should be a place where saints are being equipped, where the next generation learns what it means to build up the body of Christ. The principles of Ephesians 4:12 apply just as much around your dinner table as they do in the church gathering.

Here's the challenge: Are you building others up to depend on you, or to depend on Christ? Are the men in your group growing in their ability to minister to others, or are they just growing in their appreciation for your ministry to them? The mark of biblical leadership is not how much people need you, but how well they're equipped to serve Christ and His church when you're not around.

QUESTIONS TO SIT WITH
1. How are you currently equipping the men in your discipleship group to minister to others, not just receive ministry from you?
2. In what specific ways are you preparing to equip saints as a potential elder, rather than just serve them?
3. How will you equip your wife and future children for their own works of ministry?
4. What areas of ministry do you need to be equipped in so that you can better equip others?
