The Text

Ephesians 4:12 — "to equip the saints for the work of ministry, for building up the body of Christ"

Paul's vision for the church is breathtakingly practical: Christ has given gifts to His people not to create a hierarchy of spiritual performers, but to equip every saint for ministry. The word "equip" here carries the sense of mending nets or setting bones—it's about restoration, preparation, and making something fully functional for its intended purpose.

This verse sits at the heart of Paul's teaching on spiritual maturity. The gifted leaders mentioned in verse 11—apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors and teachers—exist not to do all the ministry while others watch, but to prepare the whole body for the work of building itself up in love. It's a vision of the church where every member is engaged, equipped, and actively participating in the mutual edification that marks spiritual adulthood.

The ultimate goal is clear: building up the body of Christ. This isn't merely organizational efficiency; it's the very means by which Christ grows His church toward the fullness of His own stature. When saints are equipped and engaged in ministry to one another, the body builds itself up in love, and we move collectively toward mature manhood in Christ.

Devotional Angle — For You, Right Now

Calvin, this text lands squarely on your shoulders in ways that should both encourage and challenge you. As you lead the Choice Men discipleship group, you're living out exactly what Paul describes here—equipping saints for ministry. But here's the question that should keep you honest: Are you equipping the men in your group to minister, or are you content to be the one who always teaches, always leads, always carries the load?

Your aspiration to eldership makes this even more pointed. The elders who will potentially examine you aren't looking for another professional minister; they're looking for someone who can multiply ministry through others. Can you point to men in Choice Men who are now ministering to others because of how you've equipped them? Are you preparing them to lead, to disciple, to serve—or are you keeping them dependent on you?

And as you prepare to become a father, this principle takes on profound personal urgency. The child coming into your home will need to be equipped for ministry, not merely cared for. Your wife will need you to equip her for the ministry of motherhood, not just support her through it. The domestic church you're building requires the same equipping dynamic Paul describes here.

Here's the uncomfortable truth: if your leadership style in Choice Men looks more like performance than preparation, if the men are more impressed with your knowledge than equipped for their own ministry, then you're missing Paul's vision entirely. The measure of your effectiveness isn't how well you teach or how much the men respect you—it's how well they're able to build up the body of Christ because you equipped them to do so.

Questions to Sit With
1.
What specific ministry skills or capacities are you actively developing in the men you disciple, and how are you measuring their growth in these areas?
2.
When you look at your leadership in Choice Men, what percentage of your time is spent performing ministry versus equipping others to minister?
3.
How will your approach to equipping your wife for ministry need to change once your child arrives, and what are you doing now to prepare for that shift?
4.
If you were examined for eldership today, what concrete examples could you give of saints you've equipped who are now actively building up the body of Christ?

A Prayer to Close

Father, you have placed me in positions of leadership not for my own glory but for the equipping of your saints. Help me to see clearly where I am building my own reputation rather than building up your people. Give me wisdom to know how to prepare the Choice Men not just to be good disciples, but to be effective disciplers of others. As I prepare for fatherhood and pursue eldership, shape my heart to value the ministry of others more than my own ministry performance. May your body be built up through the saints I equip, for the glory of Christ alone.

Today's Action: Have a conversation with one man from Choice Men about a specific ministry opportunity where he could serve others, and commit to helping him prepare for it over the next month.
