The Text
Ephesians 4:12
"to equip the saints for the work of ministry, for building up the body of Christ"

Paul's vision for the church is breathtakingly clear: Christ has given gifts to His people not for personal glory or individual advancement, but for the equipping of saints. The word "equip" here carries the sense of mending, preparing, and making ready—like a fisherman mending his nets or a surgeon preparing his instruments. The gifted leaders in the church exist to prepare God's people for their own ministry.

This equipping isn't an end in itself. Notice the progression: gifted leaders equip saints, saints do the work of ministry, and the result is the building up of Christ's body. Every believer is called to ministry, to the work of serving others and building up the church. The ultimate goal is not individual maturity alone, but the growth and strengthening of the entire body of Christ.

The phrase "building up" suggests both construction and edification—we are simultaneously building something lasting and encouraging one another in the faith. This is the church "adulting"—moving beyond spiritual infancy to take responsibility for the care and growth of others in the body.

Devotional Angle — For You, Right Now

Calvin, as you step deeper into leadership at TEC, this passage speaks directly to your calling. Leading Choice Men isn't about being the guy with all the answers—it's about equipping the men in your group to do ministry themselves. Are you teaching them to serve, or just serving them? There's a crucial difference. Your role is to prepare these men to build up others, to become ministers in their own spheres.

This vision should be transforming how you think about your aspirations to eldership. The elders at TEC aren't called to do all the ministry—they're called to equip you and others to do it. As you grow in this direction, are you developing the heart and skills of an equipper? Are you learning to see potential in others and draw it out? Your future effectiveness as an elder will be measured not by how much ministry you do, but by how well you prepare others to minister.

And Calvin, with a child on the way, this principle will soon hit home in the most personal way. You're about to become a father-equipper. Your calling will be to prepare your child for their own work of ministry, their own contribution to building up the body of Christ. The same heart that should drive your leadership in Choice Men—seeing others equipped and released into ministry—needs to permeate your approach to fatherhood.

Don't miss this: every conversation in Choice Men, every moment of pastoral care you offer, every time you help a brother grow—this is you participating in Christ's grand design for His church. You're not just leading a group; you're participating in the cosmic work of building up the body of Christ. This is adulting in the truest sense—taking responsibility for the growth and maturity of others.

A Prayer to Close

Father, thank You for the privilege of being equipped and called to equip others. Help me to see clearly that my role in Choice Men, my aspirations to eldership, and my coming fatherhood are all part of Your grand design to build up the body of Christ. Give me wisdom to know when to serve directly and when to step back and equip others to serve. Shape my heart to find joy not in being needed, but in seeing others grow and minister effectively. May everything I do contribute to the building up of Your church.
