TEC — Sunday, April 19

Serving Saints

The Adulting Church, Part 2
Preacher: Pastor Sean Text: Ephesians 4:12 Series: The Adulting Church
The Text
"To equip the saints for the work of ministry, for building up the body of Christ" (Ephesians 4:12)

Paul's vision for the church is breathtakingly practical. Christ has given gifts to His people—apostles, prophets, evangelists, pastors and teachers—not to create a clergy class that does all the work while everyone else watches, but to equip every saint for ministry. The word "equip" carries the idea of mending nets, setting bones, or preparing soldiers for battle. It's intensive, intentional work aimed at making something ready for its intended purpose.

The goal is clear: building up the body of Christ. This isn't about personal fulfillment or finding your passion, though those may be byproducts. It's about the corporate health and growth of Christ's church. Every believer has work to do in this building project, and the health of the whole body depends on each member functioning properly in love.

The mature church Paul envisions in verse 13 doesn't happen by accident. It requires saints who are equipped and actively engaged in the work of ministry, contributing to the spiritual architecture that Christ Himself is building. This is adulting in the truest sense—taking responsibility for the wellbeing of others and the advancement of God's kingdom.

Devotional Angle — For You, Right Now

Calvin, as you sit with this text today, consider the weight of what God has placed in your hands through Choice Men. You're not just leading a Bible study or facilitating discussions—you're actively participating in the equipping work that Paul describes. Every Tuesday night when those men gather, you're functioning as one of Christ's tools to prepare saints for ministry. The questions you ask, the vulnerabilities you model, the biblical truth you help them see—this is the mending, the bone-setting, the battle preparation that builds up the body.

But here's what strikes me about your current season: you're simultaneously being equipped and equipping others. As an aspiring elder, you're under the careful preparation of current elders who are doing this very work in your life. They're mending what needs mending, strengthening what needs strengthening, preparing you for greater service. Don't despise this process or rush through it. The church needs elders who have been thoroughly equipped, not just eager volunteers.

And consider this reality as you prepare for fatherhood—your future child will be born into a church where this equipping work is happening. The health of the body your child will grow up in depends partly on how seriously you take this calling now. The men you're discipling in Choice Men may one day disciple your son or daughter. The way you submit to being equipped as an elder shapes the kind of leadership your family will experience in this church.

The temptation for men in your position is to compartmentalize these roles—husband here, father there, group leader over there, aspiring elder somewhere else. But Paul's vision is integrative. You're being equipped as a whole person for the comprehensive work of building up the body. Your marriage, your approaching fatherhood, your leadership—all of it serves the same ultimate purpose of Christ building His church unto maturity.

Questions to Sit With
1.
How are you seeing the men in Choice Men grow in their capacity for ministry, and what does this reveal about your own growth as an equipper?
2.
In what specific ways are the current elders equipping you for future service, and how are you actively participating in that process?
3.
What would it look like for your marriage and approaching fatherhood to serve the building up of the body, rather than competing with it for time and attention?
4.
Where do you sense God calling you to move from being equipped to actively equipping others in new ways?

A Prayer to Close

Father, thank You for placing me in a church where the work of equipping saints is taken seriously. Help me to be faithful in both receiving and giving this equipping work—submitting well to those You've placed over me while serving faithfully those You've entrusted to my care. Shape me into the kind of leader this body needs, the kind of husband who builds up rather than tears down, and the kind of father who raises children for Your kingdom purposes. Use every aspect of my life for the building up of Your church. Amen.

Today: During lunch and the afternoon singing, intentionally connect with someone you don't know well and ask them about their story of growth in this church. Listen for how God has been equipping them for ministry.
