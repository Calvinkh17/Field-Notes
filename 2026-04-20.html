Serving Saints

The Adulting Church • Part 2
Preacher: Sean / Text: Ephesians 4:12 / Series: The Adulting Church
The Text
Ephesians 4:11-16 (ESV)
"And he gave the apostles, the prophets, the evangelists, the shepherds and teachers, to equip the saints for the work of ministry, for building up the body of Christ, until we all attain to the unity of the faith and of the knowledge of the Son of God, to mature manhood, to the measure of the stature of the fullness of Christ, so that we may no longer be children, tossed to and fro by the waves and carried about by every wind of doctrine, by human cunning, by craftiness in deceitful schemes. Rather, speaking the truth in love, we are to grow up in every way into him who is the head, into Christ, from whom the whole body, joined and held together by every joint, with which it is equipped, when each part is working properly, makes the body grow so that it builds itself up in love."

Paul's vision for the church in Ephesians 4 is breathtakingly comprehensive. Christ has given gifted leaders not as an end in themselves, but as instruments to equip every saint for ministry. The goal is not to create a professional clergy class that does ministry while everyone else watches, but to prepare the entire body for the work of building up the church.

The phrase "for the work of ministry" in verse 12 applies not just to formal church leaders, but to all the saints who are being equipped. Every Christian has a ministry to perform in building up the body of Christ. This work continues "until we all attain" to mature manhood—indicating that none of us has arrived, but all of us are called to grow and to help others grow.

The beautiful picture Paul paints is of a body where "each part is working properly" and the whole "builds itself up in love." This is not merely organizational efficiency, but organic spiritual vitality. When saints serve saints, the body grows not just in size but in spiritual maturity, moving from childish instability to Christlike maturity.

Devotional Angle — For You, Right Now

Calvin, this text speaks directly to your current season of preparation and leadership. As you lead Choice Men, you're living out Ephesians 4:12 in real time—equipping other men for ministry, building them up in Christ. But notice that your role isn't to do all the ministry yourself; it's to prepare them to do the work of ministry. Are you helping these men discover their own calling to serve, or are you carrying the load alone?

Your aspiration to eldership means you're moving toward formal recognition as one who equips the saints. But here's the reality check: serving saints is not about being the most spiritual person in the room. It's about helping others "grow up in every way into Christ." Your wife needs this from you as she prepares for motherhood. The men in your group need this as they face their own challenges. This means being less concerned with having all the answers and more focused on asking the right questions that help others discover what Christ is doing in their lives.

As a soon-to-be father, you're about to enter a whole new dimension of "building up the body." Your child will be part of the church body that needs to be equipped for ministry. This starts with how you model servant leadership in your home. Your wife is watching how you handle responsibility, pressure, and the needs of others. She needs to see in you someone who builds others up rather than tears them down, someone who speaks truth in love rather than harsh criticism or passive avoidance.

The danger for driven men like you is thinking that serving saints means having your act completely together first. But Paul's vision is of a body where "each part is working properly"—not perfectly, but properly. You serve others not from a position of having arrived, but from a posture of growing alongside them toward "mature manhood." Your Choice Men don't need a perfect leader; they need an authentic one who is committed to the long work of spiritual formation—in himself and in them.

Questions to Sit With
1.
In your leadership of Choice Men, are you equipping them for their own ministry, or are you creating dependence on your leadership?
2.
What would it look like for you to "speak the truth in love" to your wife about the changes coming with parenthood, rather than avoiding difficult conversations?
3.
How are you currently being equipped by others for ministry, and where do you need to humbly receive input rather than always giving it?
4.
What specific ways could you help one person in your circle grow spiritually this week, focusing on their development rather than your own reputation as a leader?

A Prayer to Close
Lord Jesus, You are building Your church unto maturity, and You have called me to be part of that work. Help me to serve the saints around me—my wife, the men in my group, the congregation—not from pride or self-sufficiency, but from a heart that genuinely desires their spiritual growth. Show me how to equip others for ministry rather than doing everything myself. Give me wisdom as I prepare for eldership and fatherhood, that I might speak truth in love and help build up Your body. Make me faithful in this good work until we all attain to mature manhood in You. Amen.
